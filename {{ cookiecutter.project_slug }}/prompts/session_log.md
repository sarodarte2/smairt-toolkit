## How to Use This File

For each session:
1. Record the date and session number
2. Paste your prompts exactly as you gave them
3. Summarize key points from AI responses (or paste full responses)
4. Note which scripts were generated
5. Add your own observations and thoughts

This creates a breadcrumb trail so you can feed the entire repo back to AI and it can recreate the thought process.

## Quick Reference: What to Record

From each session, capture:

1. **The prompts** - Exactly what you asked
2. **The responses** - At minimum, key points; ideally, full responses
3. **Scripts generated** - Link to the files created
4. **Results** - What happened when you ran them
5. **Your observations** - What you noticed, thought about, connected
6. **Your critical steps** - Where YOU made the key intellectual contributions

"With AI liner, I had a few different places where I made critical steps where it seemed like we were maybe at a dead end or we didn't have good ideas, and I suggested, for instance, we do the motif searching, the cross-studying attention, and comparing attention was kind of my idea as well."

---

## Tips

- **Don't edit your prompts after the fact** - Keep them as you actually wrote them
- **Note what worked "within certain boundaries"** - Document where approaches work and where they break down
- **Record when things stop working** - "They work within certain boundaries and then they stop working where they don't work for real data, or they are not robust to noise, or they are limited"
- **Feed this file back to AI** - When starting a new session, include this log so AI can recreate context
